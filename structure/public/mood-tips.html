ANGRY Anger Management Tips

  • more_vert
    • Action
    • Another action
Honor your feelings. Trying to bury the funk or pretend it's not there will only create a pressure cooker-type situation where you are likely to explode later. Instead of focusing on what made you mad, work on resolving the issue at hand. Find a solution for the time being, and let that be enough. Take a deep breath and remind yourself that anger won't fix anything and might only make it worse.

TIRED Beat Fatigue Tips

  • more_vert
    • Action
    • Another action
People frequently get in the habit of trying to do too much. The demands we put on ourselves may leave us feeling fatigued — and if there are not enough hours in the day to juggle our lives, our sleep often suffers. On the other hand, having a more balanced lifestyle, making time for yourself, and learning to relax and slow down sometimes will help you function efficiently.

STRESSED Stress Management Tips

  • info_outline
Stress-induced emotions consume huge amounts of energy. Talking with a friend or relative, joining a support group, or seeing a psychotherapist can all help diffuse stress. Relaxation therapies like meditation, self-hypnosis, yoga, and tai chi are also effective tools for reducing stress.

HAPPY Keep Smiling Tips

  • more_vert
    • Action
    • Another action
Sometimes it really does start with a happy outlook. Enjoy some funny videos online or clips from your favourite sitcoms. Not only will a good guffaw release stress, but it also enhances your oxygen intake, which stimulates your organs and endorphins. All the many benefits of laughter can be felt almost instantly. Let yourself laugh!

SAD Beat the Blues Tips

  • info_outline
Many people feel depressed during the winter months, when they travel to and from work in darkness and don't get enough natural sunlight. Physical exercise, however, encourages the release of hormones and neurochemicals that boost mood, says Richard Brown, MD. Walking outside during the day will give you a few short doses of sunlight, also shown to boost mood, particularly in the winter.

ENERGIZED Maintain Energy Tips

  • more_vert
    • Action
    • Another action
Exercise almost guarantees that you'll feel more energized and sleep more soundly. It also gives your cells more energy to burn and circulates oxygen. And exercising causes your body to release epinephrine and norepinephrine, stress hormones that in modest amounts can make you feel energized. Even a brisk walk is a good start.
